Eurobot 2026 — Autonomous Match Robot

Europe's biggest student robotics competition: two autonomous robots, 100 seconds, zero human input. I own the mechanical architecture of the IEEE RAS INSAT robots and co-engineer their control systems.

Role
Mechanical Design Engineer · Co-Control Systems Engineer
Team
IEEE RAS INSAT Student Branch
Timeline
Sep 2025 – Present
Stack
SolidWorks · Onshape · ROS 2 · STM32 · Raspberry Pi · ESP32

The challenge

Eurobot pits fully autonomous robots against each other on a shared game table. Robots must perceive the field, avoid the opponent, manipulate game elements and score — all in real time, under match pressure, with no remote control allowed. Reliability is everything: a jammed mechanism or a missed detection ends the match.

My contribution

  • Full mechanical architecture of the main robot, from concept to fabricated prototype — parametric 3D modeling and complex assemblies in SolidWorks and Onshape, designed for fast iteration and manufacturability.
  • Actuation systems design: motors, transmissions and mechanical linkages for the crate pick-up and rotation arms and the deployable precision-cursor hand mechanism, engineered in coordination with the control strategies.
  • Co-engineering the control stack: working with the electronics and software teams on ROS 2-based autonomy — LiDAR-based opponent detection, dynamic map movement and robust autonomous match execution.
  • Prototyping and validation loops that systematically reduced mechanical failure points through iterative testing before competition.

The machines

  • Main robot — LiDAR perception, dual robotic arms for crate handling, deployable fine-positioning mechanism, powered by a Raspberry Pi + STM32 + custom actuator PCBs (ESP32).
  • SIMA — compact mobile actuators built around an STM32 Blue Pill with a custom shield PCB and two 12V 1000 RPM motors with rotary encoders.
  • Hardware stack: 8 servo motors, Pololu Maestro card, custom STM32 motor-driver shield, custom power PCB, 2 × 24V encoder motors.
